## 4. Pairwise Correlation Analysis

### 4.1 Our framework vs BeZero (n=27 paired projects)

Using all 27 projects where we have both our framework score and a BeZero rating (all 30 minus EXP11, EXP12 which lack BeZero, and EXP28 which has only Calyx).

**Computed Spearman rho: +0.901**
**Computed Kendall tau-b: +0.821**
**Exact grade match (mapped): 14/27 (52%)**
**Within ±1 grade: 27/27 (100%)**

This is a substantial improvement over the REDD+-only correlation (+0.664 on n=6) and consistent with the earlier n=18 result (+0.891). The full dataset spans B-to-AAA on our scale and D-to-AAA on BeZero's across 12 project types, providing wide dynamic range. Every project is within one grade of the mapped BeZero rating, demonstrating systematic tracking across the quality spectrum.

See `expanded_correlation.json` for the full numeric dataset and `new_scores.md` for per-project scoring rationale.

### 4.2 Our framework vs Calyx (n=8 scored projects)

Using the 8 projects with our score and a Calyx rating (EXP01-06 using NZM scale, EXP11-12 using new scale):

The REDD+ subset (EXP01-06) shows near-zero or negative correlation (Calyx gave E to 5 of 6 projects). The cookstove subset shows systematic divergence (we give BBB, Calyx gives A/AA). Sample too heterogeneous in scale for a single Spearman to be meaningful -- the NZM 5-point scale for REDD+ and the new AAA-D scale for cookstoves are not directly comparable.

### 4.3 Our framework vs Sylvera (n=7 scored projects)

The 7 projects with Sylvera data (EXP01-06 using NZM tiers, EXP09 using letter grade) show:
- EXP09 (Exomad Green): our AA = Sylvera AA (exact match)
- EXP01-06: mixed results per the original REDD+ analysis

### 4.4 Cross-agency: BeZero vs MSCI (n=1)

EXP24 (Heartland 3): BeZero BB vs MSCI AA. Only one data point but notable for the large disagreement -- suggests fundamentally different treatment of orphan-well methane abatement risk.

## 7. Data Collection Notes

### 7.1 BeZero (26 of 30 projects)
BeZero publishes all headline ratings publicly at bezerocarbon.com/ratings/listings (677+ projects as of Apr 2026). This is the most extensive public disclosure of any agency. Our dataset captures projects where the specific rating is documented in press releases, case studies, or their public listings page.

### 7.2 Calyx Global (10 of 30 projects)
Calyx opened its platform to public access in June 2025, but granular project-level ratings (AAA-D) remain subscriber-only. Our Calyx data comes from: (a) CMW 2023 Table 20 (NZM scale, 6 REDD+ projects), (b) public research reports naming specific projects with ratings (BURN/Key Carbon AA, Arborify A, C-Quest Capital E), and (c) the CCP label tracker.

### 7.3 Sylvera (7 of 30 projects)
Sylvera's project-level ratings are largely behind a paywall. Our data comes from: (a) CMW 2023 Table 20 (NZM tier scale, 6 REDD+ projects), and (b) the Exomad Green press release (AA). Sylvera launched biochar ratings in late 2024 and may have additional public data points for biochar projects.

### 7.4 MSCI (1 of 30 projects)
MSCI Carbon Project Ratings cover 4,400+ projects but are paywalled. The only public project-level rating found is Rebellion Heartland 3 (AA), disclosed via press release.

### 7.5 Selection bias
This dataset is biased toward high-profile and higher-quality projects. Low-quality projects (D/C ratings) are underrepresented because developers do not publicize poor ratings. The REDD+ subset has better representation of the low end (Kariba D, Cordillera Azul C, Brazil Nut Concessions C) from investigative journalism and watchdog reports.
