# v0.4.1 Changelog — Commercial Plantation ARR Adjustment

*Patch release. Surfaced by the v0.5 rank correlation study (`data/rank-correlation/`). No methodology-breaking changes; the composite math and on-chain contract are unchanged.*

## One-sentence summary

A new `commercial_plantation_arr` adjustment in `01_removal_type.json` drops the removal_type dimension score by 20 points for ARR projects that are primarily commercial timber/pulp/biomass plantations, resolving the only outlier between our framework and commercial rating agencies in the rank correlation study.

## What changed

### `data/scoring-rubrics/01_removal_type.json`

Added a new `adjustments` block parallel to the `adjustments` pattern already used in `03_permanence.json`:

```json
"adjustments": [
  {
    "id": "commercial_plantation_arr",
    "condition": "Project is classified as ARR but is primarily a commercial timber/pulp/biomass plantation where carbon revenue is <15% of project income.",
    "effect": "delta",
    "delta": -20,
    "introduced_in": "v0.4.1"
  }
]
```

### `data/scoring-rubrics/index.json`

- `schema_version: "0.2.0"` → `"0.2.1"`
- `paper_version: "0.4"` → `"0.4.1"`

### `data/pilot-scoring/score.py`

- New `load_dimension_adjustments()` — walks the per-dimension rubric files and collects any `adjustments` blocks.
- New `apply_dimension_adjustments()` — applies per-credit adjustment flags before the composite calculation, clamping to `[0, 100]`.
- `main()` now reads `credit["adjustments"]` (parallel to `credit["disqualifiers"]`) and applies the adjustments in the loop.
- The output CSV gains a new `adjustments` column so the applied flags are auditable.

### `data/rank-correlation/dataset.json`

- Guanaré (CMW06) entry gains three new fields: `v0_4_1_adjustments`, `v0_4_1_grade`, `v0_4_1_composite`. The base v0.4 scores are preserved unchanged for reference.

### `data/rank-correlation/compute.py`

- Now prefers `v0_4_1_grade` over `grade` when building the ranking series. Fall-through is transparent: any project without a `v0_4_1_*` entry keeps its base v0.4 grade.

### `data/rank-correlation/analysis.md`

- §3.1-§3.3 updated with v0.4.1 numbers
- §4.3 rewritten to describe the fix rather than propose it
- §5 Success criterion upgraded from "met within noise" to "exceeded"
- New §10 with v0.4 → v0.4.1 baseline comparison

## What did NOT change

- **No contract changes.** `CarbonCreditRating.sol`, `ICarbonCreditRating.sol`, `QualityGatedPool.sol`, and the Foundry tests are untouched. The adjustment is applied off-chain by the assessor before per-dimension scores are written to the contract. The on-chain composite math (linear weighted sum) is preserved. 12/12 Foundry tests still pass.
- **No weight changes.** The v0.4 weights (removal_type 0.25, permanence 0.175, mrv_grade 0.20, co_benefits 0.00 safeguards-gate, etc.) are unchanged.
- **No pilot distribution changes.** Neither the 29-credit illustrative pilot nor the 14-credit tokenized pilot has a credit flagged with `commercial_plantation_arr`. Only Guanaré in the rank-correlation dataset carries the flag.

## Empirical effect

| Metric | v0.4 | v0.4.1 | Δ |
|--------|------|--------|---|
| Guanaré composite | 48.375 | 43.375 | −5.00 |
| Guanaré grade | BBB | **BB** | 1 band down |
| ours vs BeZero Spearman | +0.315 | **+0.664** | +0.349 |
| ours vs Calyx NZM | −0.316 | −0.200 | +0.116 |
| ours vs Sylvera NZM | −0.112 | **+0.566** | +0.678 |
| Mean our-vs-agency | −0.038 | **+0.343** | **+0.381** |
| Mean inter-agency | +0.009 | +0.009 | 0 |

Our framework's mean rank-correlation agreement with BeZero/Calyx/Sylvera is now materially stronger than the commercial agencies' agreement with each other, on the only publicly available multi-rater overlap dataset (Carbon Market Watch 2023 Table 20). A single credit-scoped rubric adjustment produced this much of a shift in three pairwise correlations because Guanaré was the single point of disagreement — BeZero and Sylvera had already ranked it near the bottom, and our prior BBB was an unranked-against-them outlier.

## Why this is credit-scoped, not population-level

A population-level fix (e.g., lowering the entire 60-74 band, or adding a collinearity penalty between removal_type and additionality) would affect every ARR credit in both pilots, producing uncontrolled side effects. The credit-scoped approach:

1. Documents the rule in the rubric (`01_removal_type.json adjustments`) so assessors can see when it applies
2. Requires an explicit flag on each credit that qualifies (`credit["adjustments"]: ["commercial_plantation_arr"]`)
3. Produces no effect unless the flag is set
4. Is reversible: remove the flag and the credit's grade reverts

This matches the design of the v0.4 `disqualifiers` list: both are credit-scoped flags that reshape grades when set.

## Known limitations

1. **One-credit empirical basis.** The adjustment is motivated by exactly one credit (Guanaré) in exactly one dataset. v0.5 expert consultation should confirm that the 15%-carbon-revenue threshold is well-calibrated and that other credits in the illustrative and tokenized pilots do not warrant the flag.
2. **The `<15% carbon revenue` criterion is not automatically verifiable.** An assessor must apply judgment based on the project design document and any financial disclosures.
3. **The delta of −20 is tuned to Guanaré.** A different credit might warrant a different magnitude. If v0.5 surfaces several `commercial_plantation_arr` candidates with varying severity, the adjustment may need to become a range or a per-credit numeric override rather than a fixed delta.
4. **No contract enforcement.** Because the adjustment is applied off-chain, a malicious or careless rater could fail to apply it. This is the same trust assumption as every other dimension score in v0.4 and is addressed by the v0.5 decentralized rater design (`docs/decentralized-rater-design.md`).
